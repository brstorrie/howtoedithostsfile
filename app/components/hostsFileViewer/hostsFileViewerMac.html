Mac OS X

  1. Open Spotlight (Command + Space, or the magnifying glass in the top right), type "terminal" and press enter
  2. Enter the command 'sudo vi /etc/hosts' (without quotes), press enter. You will be prompted for your password
  3. Enter your password, and press enter. You will be presented with the default hosts file.
  4. Press "i" to enter insert mode in vim, and use your arrow keys to move to a blank line, and enter the source and desired alias, separated by a space. (Usually an IP address, followed by a url-style alias, as shown below)
  5. Press Esc, then :wq, followed by Enter to write and save changes. After that is complete, ping your new alias to ensure that it is resolving to the new IP address!
  6. That's it! In this example we've used a typical router IP address, but any IP address can be used
